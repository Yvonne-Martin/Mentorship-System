BORN A CRIME

SUMMARY OF CHAPTER 1 AND CHAPTER 2

CHARACTERS

TREVOR NOAH THE AUTHOR/NARRATOR

TREVOR’S MOM

1. RACISM

In chapters 1&2 of the story Born a Crime, Trevor Noah describes his childhood life. He was born in South Africa in the year 1984 during a period when the black South Africans were divided into tribes. Trevor Noah narrates how he lived during the years before and the end of the apartheid system. These tribes had a history of conflict where the Zulu and the Xhosa were the most dominant tribes during the apartheid period. (Apartheid is the legal system for racial segregation). The Zulu were mostly known as the warriors while the Xhosa were known as the intelligent. This is a major issue of racism where some tribes are not allowed to have sexual relationships with another tribe. Trevor was born to a black mother and a white father which was illegal and would be punishable by the law. This issue is visible in Machakos County where people from high social classes are not allowed to interact with people from low social classes and also in Ukambani, people from a particular clan are not allowed to intermarry with people from a particular clan since it is forbidden and is punishable.

2. CHRISTIANITY

Trevor’s mother was so much into Christianity and she would take the whole family to multiple churches. She claimed that each church offered something different from the rest of the churches where they had some bit of racial demographics. Trevor goes ahead to say that he enjoyed going to church to watch the pastor cast demons out of people. These churches were the black church, the white church, and the mixed church. Trevor’s mother would never be intimidated by anything so easily since her faith in God was unwavering. They would have to attend the church no matter what comes their way. On one occasion, their car broke down and they had to board a minibus. Trevor kept on insisting that maybe the Lord did not want them to go to church on that particular day but that could not stop the mother from attending the church services. This is evident in Machakos County where people believe in going to different churches for spiritual growth. Whereas others just go to church for fun to watch when the spiritual teachers are casting demons out of people.
